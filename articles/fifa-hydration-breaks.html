FIFA's mandatory 'Hydration Breaks' spark intense World Cup controversy.

For the first time in tournament history, FIFA has mandated three-minute hydration breaks midway through each half of the 2026 World Cup to protect players from extreme North American heat.

While sports scientists praise the move for player safety, the breaks have sparked massive controversy among purists and tacticians. Managers are actively using the three-minute pause as a 'timeout' to completely restructure their formations, drawing complaints that the traditional continuous flow of football is being Americanized.
